# Nana Rose — Story Bible
## ClearPop / Cozy Kitchen

---

## The Premise

**Nana Rose** is a cozy kitchen witch who runs a beloved neighborhood bakery. She bakes everything herself, from scratch, every single day — enchanted pastries, charmed cookies, spell-infused cakes. Her regulars line up before she opens.

The only problem is her pantry.

Years ago, Nana cast a preservation spell on it — meant to keep her ingredients fresh forever. She misread a line. Now, every night while she sleeps, all her ingredients crystallize into glowing colored **charms** that stack and cluster on the pantry shelves. Every morning she has to break them apart by hand to release what's locked inside before she can bake a single thing.

She's been meaning to fix it for thirty years. She probably never will.

*"Same thing every morning. One of these days I'll sort it out."*

---

## The Loop

```
Café gets an order
  → Nana goes to the pantry
    → Player clears the charm clusters
      → Ingredients are released
        → Nana bakes the order
          → Interstitial: the finished dish, served
```

The board is the **pantry**. The colored blocks are **charms** — crystallized ingredients. Clearing a group breaks the charm and releases what's inside. The refill from the top is the pantry churning out more overnight charm buildup.

---

## The Charms (Colored Blocks)

Charms are Nana's own ingredients, crystallized by the botched spell. They appear in 3 colors per level, chosen by the pantry's mood. The player doesn't pick what they get — that's the whole problem.

**Color palette:** warm, food-adjacent — amber, rose, sage, cream, plum. Rounded, glowing, slightly translucent. Think candied gems or stained glass sugar.

Each charm cluster broken = a small burst of the ingredient inside escaping. The bigger the clear, the bigger the release — which is why large clears produce spells (see Power-ups).

---

## The Pantry Obstacles (Blockers)

These are things that make the morning harder — other kitchen mishaps that have accumulated on the shelves alongside the charm buildup.

| Obstacle | Pantry Name | What It Is | HP | Behavior |
|----------|-------------|------------|-----|----------|
| Cookie | **Hex Cookie** | A batch Nana baked that absorbed too much magic and hardened to stone. Has to be crumbled. | 3 | Crumbles in stages (whole → bitten → dust) |
| Egg | **Pantry Egg** | Eggs that got caught in the overnight charm buildup and stuck to the shelves. | 2 | Cracks then crumbles (two stages) |
| Marshmallow | **Puff Hex** | Old spells that didn't dissolve properly — they just puffed up and stuck to shelves. | 2 | Deflates in two stages |
| Jelly | **Spell Residue** | Sticky magical runoff from old potions. One firm tap and it dissolves. | 1 | Pops in one hit |
| Cage → **Vine Tangle** | **Vine Tangle** | Enchanted herb vines that crept off the spice rack overnight. | 1 | Snipped in one hit |
| Safe → **Grimoire Box** | **Grimoire Box** | A sealed magical container Nana uses for special ingredients. Takes two hits to unlock. | 2 | Cracks the seal on first hit, opens on second |

## The Spells (Power-ups)

Large charm clears release a burst of concentrated magic. These become usable spells — Nana's tricks for dealing with a stubborn pantry morning.

| Power-up | Spell Name | Threshold | Effect | Nana's Line |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Pepper | **Pepper Hex** | 5+ group | Clears an entire row or column — a streak of heat across the shelf | *"A little heat solves everything."* |
| Popcorn | **Pop Jinx** | 7+ group | Clears a 3×3 area — pops in all directions | *"That one's always satisfying."* |
| Candy | **Sweet Sweep** | 11+ group | Clears every charm of one color — enchants them all at once | *"The pantry always had a sweet tooth."* |

**Combos** are called **double-casts** — two spells landing at once produce outsized magic.

## Nana Rose — Character

**Who she is:** Warm, a little exasperated, completely unimpressed by magic. She's been a witch long enough that the wonder wore off — now it's just Tuesday. She loves her bakery, loves her customers, tolerates her pantry.

**Voice:** Dry, affectionate, never panicked. She talks to the pantry like a misbehaving pet she's too fond of to discipline properly.

**Visuals:**
- Apron over a cozy dress
- Pointy hat (small, worn, tilted — not theatrical)
- Reading glasses she pushes up when concentrating
- Wooden spoon tucked behind one ear (also her wand)
- Silver hair, warm complexion, laugh lines

**Expressions needed (talking head, portrait format):**
1. **Neutral/ready** — arms crossed, slight smile, expectant
2. **Pleased** — genuine warm smile, nodding
3. **Exasperated** — eyes closed, one hand raised ("I know, I know")
4. **Surprised/delighted** — eyebrows up, spoon out

## The Café

**Name:** The Charm & Crumb

A small neighborhood bakery. The regulars know about the magic — it's the neighborhood, everyone knows. Mismatched cups, herbs hanging in the window, a chalkboard menu that rewrites itself.

## Tone Reference

- **Cozy, not saccharine.** Warmth with dry wit underneath.
- **Magic is mundane.** It's not a fantasy epic — it's just how Tuesday works.
- **The pantry is a character.** It has moods. Nana has opinions about them.
- **The bakery is real.** The customers matter. The orders have emotional stakes.

**Comparable tones:** *Kiki's Delivery Service*, *Practical Magic*, *The Bear* (without the stress), early *Stardew Valley* NPC writing.
